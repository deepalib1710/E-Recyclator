Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about electronic waste management and recycling. Learn how to responsibly dispose of your old devices and understand the impact of proper e-waste handling.

What exactly constitutes e-waste?

E-waste (electronic waste) includes any discarded electrical or electronic devices. Common examples include:

  • Computers & laptops
  • Smartphones & tablets
  • Televisions & monitors
  • Printers & peripherals
  • Batteries & power banks
  • Gaming consoles
  • Small kitchen appliances

Why is improper e-waste disposal dangerous?

E-waste contains numerous toxic substances that pose significant environmental and health risks:

Lead (~40% of e-waste)

Damages nervous systems and causes developmental issues in children

Mercury (3000 tons/year)

Contaminates water supplies and causes neurological damage

Cadmium (22% of exposure)

Accumulates in kidneys and liver, causing organ failure

When burned or landfilled, these toxins release dioxins that can travel up to 25 miles through air currents.

How is data security handled during recycling?

Certified recyclers follow NIST 800-88 standards for data destruction:

  • Physical destruction of storage media
  • 3-pass DoD wiping for reusable drives
  • Encrypted data log documentation
  • Chain-of-custody tracking

All partners maintain ISO 27001 information security certification
